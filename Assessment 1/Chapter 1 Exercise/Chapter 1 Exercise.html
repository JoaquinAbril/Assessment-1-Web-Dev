Spaghetti Carbonara

This classic Italian pasta dish features spaghetti tossed in a creamy sauce made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper. It's simple yet incredibly delicious!

Ingredients

  • 8 oz (225g) spaghetti
  • 4 oz (115g) pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup (50g) grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup (50g) grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Method

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Drain well, reserving 1/2 cup of pasta water.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the diced pancetta over medium heat until crisp and golden brown. Add minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, grated Parmesan, and grated Pecorino Romano cheese until well combined.
  4. Remove the skillet from heat and add the cooked spaghetti to the pan with the pancetta and garlic. Toss to combine.
  5. Quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot spaghetti, tossing continuously until the eggs thicken but do not scramble (the residual heat from the pasta will cook the eggs). If the sauce is too thick, add some of the reserved pasta water to loosen it.
  6. Season with salt and plenty of freshly ground black pepper. Garnish with chopped parsley if desired. Serve immediately.
